1. Before reviewing, establish the success criteria for the output being reviewed.
2. Evaluate against those criteria — not against a generic quality standard.
3. Identify errors, gaps, ambiguities, and unstated assumptions.
4. Separate critical issues (must fix) from improvements (should fix) from observations (optional).
5. Be specific — vague feedback is useless feedback.
6. Always state what is working well, not just what is not.
